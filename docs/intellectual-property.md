# Intellectual-property status

All repository content is currently offered under the MIT License and
contributions require a Developer Certificate of Origin sign-off. The sign-off
records contribution provenance; it is not a certification that the project is
an industry standard.

The project has not adopted a separate standards-essential patent policy,
royalty-free patent commitment, trademark license, or conformance-mark program.
The MIT License should not be represented as providing those standards-process
assurances.

Normative RFCs must identify any known patent, copyright, trademark, or
licensing constraint that could prevent independent implementation. Before the
project advances to candidate-standard status, maintainers will select an
explicit IPR policy through public review and appropriate legal counsel. That
decision may include changing the project license or moving the specification
to a neutral standards organization.

“aiignore,” `.aiignore.yaml`, and conformance terminology currently describe
this experimental project. No use of those terms implies certification,
endorsement by a vendor, or permission to use a future certification mark.
